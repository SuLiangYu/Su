Feeling all the feels - Amazon Music's new emotional features

UI/UX Design Team : LiangYu Su, Anne Kuo, Esther Kim, Jeany Lee

Tools : Figma, Miro

Project Time : 2023.02 - 2023.03

Project Brief

Amazon music aims to create a near future music service for world-wide users. Our team target to Gen-Z listeners, delevoped a series of experience design mainly provide users with emotional features: Emotion Detection, Recommended playlists based on users' emotion/mood, Listening/Emotion history tracking and Social Interaction features.

Interview Insight

Based on our survey and interview, we created 4 personas and listed out their common needs and frustrations.

“ I forgot the songs I want to listen to, I just remember that day is a sad day ”

Hard to recall the music played in a certain mood

Users might recall that they were sad last week but forgot the exact date. The sad music they discovered that day is therefore hard to be found again.

“ Sometimes the recommendation fit my music taste, but sometimes not ”

Recommended songs do not align with their tastes

Recommended songs sometimes don’t fit into the type of music they are looking for. A Swiftie looking for energetic songs might be disappointed hearing hip-hop in the playlist.

“ Sharing songs with my friends through other apps, it's a bit annoying ”

Switching between apps when sharing songs

As Gen-z often uses the sharing option to share songs and lyrics with their friends, it’s a hassle for them to jump back and forth between multiple apps to paste links and images.

Ideation

With our audience and their pain points in mind, we drafted our ideas based on these three themes:

1. Emotional & Environmental Detection

How might we detect users’ emotions and environment so they can receive songs that match their current situations?
How might we give users more control on recommended content?

2. Personalization

How might we reflect users’ personal emotional journeys?
How might we tailor the interface so users can access their favorite music in a blink of an eye?

3. Social Interactions

How might we make sharing songs with friends intuitive and convenient?
How might we connect users with their friends based on what they are listening?

Takeaways

Personlization Design

One key takeaway about personalization design is that it is essential to understand your audience and their needs in order to create effective personalized experiences. This requires gathering and analyzing data about your users, such as their demographics, behaviors, preferences, and past interactions with your product or service. Second, it's important to continually evaluate and iterate on your personalization design to ensure that it is meeting the needs of your users and providing them with value. This may involve conducting user testing, analyzing metrics, and making adjustments based on feedback and insights. Overall, personalization design can be a powerful tool for enhancing user experiences, but it requires a thoughtful and strategic approach.
