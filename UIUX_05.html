The Girl Scout Website Redeign Project to Increase Caregivers' Interest

UI/UX Design Team : LiangYu Su, Esther Kim, Tess Porter, Jiacheng Chen, Jiayi Jiang

Tools : Figma, Miro, Optimal Workshop, Slack, Adobe illustrator

Project Time : 2022.08 - 2022.12

Project Brief

The Girl Scouts provides diverse programs to develop girls' skills and personality traits. Users of the website includes children, caregivers, volunteers and staff. Due to our observation, caregivers are main users of the website, they are key people to the enrollment of the Girl Scouts. Therefore, We stood from caregivers' view to rethink the whole website, used several UX research methods to find out the potential problems of the current website. In the end of the project, we mainly focus on condensing information and adjusting structure of labels to make information which caregivers pay attention to, easily be found and reviewed. Also, added images and attractive graphic to enhance the joyful experience for browsing the website.

Part 1. Discover Problems

Interview & Survey

What do caregivers value about the girl scouts?

From the user interview and survey, we have formulated some insights:

1. Promotion through Word of Mouth

4/4 of the Interviewees received information about extracurricular programs such as the Girl Scouts from friends or colleagues, or their children heard from their classmates.

2. Importance of Children's Voices

3/4 of the interviewees thought children’s interest is the most important reason for them to decide if let their children join a program or not.

3. Complementary Relationship between Visuals and Text

3/4 of the Interviewees mentioned that they like to receive information through a mixture of visuals and text. Moreover, if a piece of information can also provide links to more information that would be helpful.

4. Clarity of Program's Vision

2/4 of the interviewees value program’s vision, and they search on the website to see if the program’s vision is aligned with what they are looking for.

Solutions

Add a section of testimony on the home page, provides caregivers a place to see what others caregivers' thoughts of the Girl Scouts.

Put children's joyful pictures of joining the programs as banners, let them know the Girl Scouts can bring positive impacts for their children.

Condense information and balance the portion of words and images.

Put the vision of the Girl Scouts at the obvious place, let caregivers find the information easier.

Persona

How do our target users look like?

Based on the research, I consolidated the keypoint findings and created a persona to help team members having the same image of our target users.

Competitive Analysis

What can we learned from others?

We compared eight main features among 6 different websites which include Homepage, Navigation, Organization, Links/Labels, Appearance, Search, Content and Mobile-Friendliness. And sorted out the advantages for us to implement and the disadvantages to avoid.

Implement

1. Show vision clearly on the home page, to let people know what they value.

2. Use good visual hierarchy and information structure to highlight important information.

3. Using proper label names for navigation.

4. Highlight the pages that are active (location of user).

5. Emphasize navigation options to learn about programs and joining.

6. In mobile, implement simple navigation that mirrors desktop website with easy-to-read font sizes and colors.
